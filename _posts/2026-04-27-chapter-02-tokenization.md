Monday 6:45 am TerraMind research spaces

The screen cursor blinked its 60 hz carrier wave and Gabriel thought about his project. The TerraMind spaces where his cubicle sat was not the 30th floor. Management had its quarterly review meetings on Mondays and Gabe was behind schedule.
Gabriel sat in the dim light of the sub-basement. The air was thick with the hum of the Milton Cluster. The server racks were labeled with faded Dymo tapes: Lumbergh, Smykowski, and there, in the corner, chugging along with a slightly misaligned cooling fan, was Milton.
Gabriel liked Milton. It was the "junk" cluster—the one management forgot to upgrade because it was tucked behind a structural pillar. It was quiet. It was steady. And, unknown to the suits on the 30th floor, it had become the nursery for a god.
